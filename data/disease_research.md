# Disease Research — Female-Skewed Prevalence

Five conditions where prevalence (or lifetime risk) is meaningfully higher in women than men, drawn from credible sources (PubMed Central, CDC NCHS, and peer-reviewed journals). Categories span autoimmune, musculoskeletal, neurological, neurodegenerative and mental health.

---

## 1. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (Lupus) — Autoimmune
- **What it is:** A chronic autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks the body's own tissues (skin, joints, kidneys, heart, brain).
- **Female-to-male ratio:** **9:1**
- **So-what:** Nine out of every ten people living with lupus are women.
- **Citation:** *Sex- and gender-related differences in systemic lupus erythematosus: a scoping review* (PMC, 2024).
- **Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12204902/

## 2. Osteoporosis — Musculoskeletal
- **What it is:** Silent bone-thinning that makes fractures much more likely after age 50 — often discovered only after a broken hip or wrist.
- **Female-to-male ratio:** **4.5:1** (19.6% of US women ≥50 vs 4.4% of men, age-adjusted)
- **So-what:** Women over 50 carry roughly 4.5× the diagnosed osteoporosis risk of men.
- **Citation:** Sarafrazi N, Wambogo EA, Shepherd JA. *Osteoporosis or Low Bone Mass in Older Adults: United States, 2017–2018.* NCHS Data Brief No. 405 (CDC, 2021).
- **Source:** https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db405.htm

## 3. Migraine — Neurological
- **What it is:** Recurring, often disabling neurological attacks involving nausea and light/sound sensitivity that can last for days — not "just a headache."
- **Female-to-male ratio:** **3:1** (lifetime: 43% women vs 18% men in the AMPP study)
- **So-what:** Migraine is the leading cause of disability for women under 50 worldwide.
- **Citation:** Buse DC et al. *Sex differences in the prevalence, symptoms, and associated features of migraine — AMPP Study.* PubMed (2013).
- **Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23808666/

## 4. Alzheimer's Disease — Neurodegenerative
- **What it is:** A progressive brain disease that destroys memory and thinking — the most common cause of dementia.
- **Female-to-male ratio:** **~2:1** (lifetime risk from age 45: 1 in 5 women vs 1 in 10 men)
- **So-what:** By age 45, a woman's lifetime risk of Alzheimer's is 1 in 5 — twice a man's.
- **Citation:** *Alzheimer's disease as a women's health challenge* (PMC, 2024).
- **Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11106001/

## 5. Major Depression — Mental Health
- **What it is:** A persistent mood disorder that affects how a person feels, thinks and handles daily life — not the same as ordinary sadness.
- **Female-to-male ratio:** **2:1**
- **So-what:** Twice as many women as men live with depression — in nearly every country measured.
- **Citation:** Albert PR. *Why is depression more prevalent in women?* Journal of Psychiatry & Neuroscience. PMC (2015).
- **Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4478054/

## Hook anchor — the diabetes thread from Zambia

In Zambia's 2017 national STEPS survey, **women were 24% more likely than men to carry multiple non-communicable disease risk factors**, and sub-studies have put female diabetes prevalence at **3.0% vs 2.1% in men**.

- **Citation:** Pengpid S, Peltzer K. *Prevalence and correlates of multiple non-communicable disease risk factors among adults in Zambia: results of the first national STEPS survey in 2017.* PMC (2021).
- **Source:** https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7864270/

The diabetes finding from Zambia is not an outlier — it sits inside a much wider pattern of conditions where women carry the heavier burden.
