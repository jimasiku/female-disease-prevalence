# Story Structure — Storytelling with Data

## Big Idea (one sentence)

> **The same female-skewed health burden that shows up in Zambia's diabetes numbers runs through five of the most disabling diseases of modern life — and the world still designs medicine, research and screening as if it didn't.**

This is a unique POV (it reframes diabetes from a country-level finding into a global pattern), it states what is at stake (millions of women under-diagnosed, under-researched and under-treated), and it is a complete sentence.

---

## 3-Minute Story Outline

### 1. Opening Hook (≈25 seconds)
> "When the Diabesity Zambia work showed that women in Zambia carry more diabetes risk than men, it sounded like a local story. It isn't."

Anchor on the Zambia STEPS 2017 finding: **women were 24% more likely than men to carry multiple non-communicable disease risk factors; female diabetes prevalence 3.0% vs 2.1% in men**. Land the question: *what if diabetes is the tip of the iceberg?*

### 2. The Tension (≈30 seconds)
For most of medical history the "default patient" was male. That default still shapes which symptoms get studied, which drugs get tested, and which screening starts when. But the disease burden does not split evenly. In five of the most disabling chronic conditions — across autoimmune, bone, brain, neurological and mental health — women carry a heavier load.

### 3. The Reveal (≈90 seconds — the data)
Five conditions, ratio of female-to-male prevalence (or lifetime risk):

| # | Condition | Category | F:M Ratio |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Lupus (SLE) | Autoimmune | **9:1** |
| 2 | Osteoporosis | Musculoskeletal | **4.5:1** |
| 3 | Migraine | Neurological | **3:1** |
| 4 | Alzheimer's disease | Neurodegenerative | **2:1** |
| 5 | Major depression | Mental health | **2:1** |

Walk slide-by-slide: one stat, one so-what, move on. No bullets.

### 4. The So-What / Call to Action (≈35 seconds)
If a single sex carries this much more of the burden, the system needs to act like it. Three concrete asks:
1. **Sex-disaggregated reporting** as a default in every NCD surveillance report — including Zambia's next STEPS round.
2. **Earlier and broader screening** for women on the conditions above, especially bone density (osteoporosis) and cognitive markers (Alzheimer's).
3. **Funding parity** for research into female-predominant diseases proportional to their burden — not their historical visibility.

**Close on the Big Idea, restated.**
